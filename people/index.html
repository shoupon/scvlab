Nicholas F. Maxemchuk (Principle Investigator)

nick@ee.columbia.edu

Office: 809 Shapiro CEPSR

Phone: 212-854-0580

Nick Maxemchuk received the B.S.E.E. degree from the City College of New York, and the M.S.E.E. and Ph.D. degrees from the University of Pennsylvania.

For the past 12 years he has been a professor in the electrical engineering department at Columbia University in New York City. He has a dual appointment as a research professor in IMDEA Networks in Madrid. Prior to joining Columbia he spent 25 years at Bell Labs and AT&T Labs and 8 years at the RCA David Sarnoff Research Center.

He has been the editor-in-chief of the IEEE Journal on Selected Areas in Communications, an editor for the IEEE Transactions on Communications, and the JACM, and was on the founding committee and steering committee of the IEEE/ACM Transactions on Networking.

In 2006 he received the Koji Kobayashi Award for his work in computer communications. He has also been awarded the IEEE's Leonard G. Abraham Prize Paper Award in 1985 and 1987,and the William R. Bennett Prize Paper Award in 1997. He has been a fellow of the IEEE since 1986.

Yitian Gu (Ph.D. Candidate)

yitian@ee.columbia.edu

Office: 807 Shapiro CEPSR

Yitian Gu received the B.S. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Shanghai, China, and the M.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from Columbia University in the City of New York. She is currently working towards the Ph.D degree from Columbia University. Her research interests include protocol design, synchronous operations and conformance testing techniques.
